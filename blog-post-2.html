What Debate Taught Me (That Biology Never Did)

Learning to listen harder, argue better, and think on my feet.

I joined Lincoln-Douglas debate thinking it would be all logic and evidence — and don’t get me wrong, it is. But what I didn’t expect was how much it would change the way I see the world, and myself.

Debate taught me how to speak with precision under pressure. It taught me that being right isn’t enough — you also have to be persuasive. Some of the most valuable lessons came from losses, when judges gave brutally honest feedback that made me rethink everything from structure to tone.

More than anything, debate gave me confidence. Not the loud, look-at-me kind — but the quiet confidence of knowing I can defend my ideas, challenge others respectfully, and speak with clarity in a room full of people. And honestly, that’s a life skill I’ll carry well beyond the next tournament.
