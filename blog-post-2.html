Why I Love LD Debate

Learning to think fast, speak clearly, and lose gracefully.

When I first joined Lincoln-Douglas debate, I was intimidated by the jargon, the speed, and the idea of arguing for things I didn’t always agree with. But that’s what made it fun.

LD isn't about yelling or memorizing arguments — it's about logic, empathy, and structure. I quickly learned how to break down a resolution, anticipate my opponent’s case, and build my own argument in real time.

One of my most valuable experiences was actually a loss. I had prepped for weeks and still got blindsided by a judge’s unexpected interpretation. At first, I was frustrated. But that round taught me to adapt, stay composed, and not tie my self-worth to a ballot.

Now, I compete not just to win, but to improve. Every round sharpens my mind — and honestly, it’s made me a better student, writer, and thinker outside of debate too.

I’ve still got a lot to learn, but I’m grateful LD pushed me out of my comfort zone. If you want to grow as a communicator — jump in.
