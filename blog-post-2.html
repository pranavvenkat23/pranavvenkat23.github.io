Why I Love LD Debate

How a side activity turned into one of my most formative passions.

Published July 2025

I didn’t expect to love debate. I joined Lincoln-Douglas partly out of curiosity, partly because a friend dragged me to a meeting. But after my first practice round — nerves, logic, rebuttals and all — I was hooked.

LD is a 1-on-1 format built around values, logic, and philosophical clash. It taught me how to listen, how to think under pressure, and how to articulate big ideas with clarity. I started winning rounds. Then tournaments. Then getting ranked.

But even more than the trophies, I’ve learned to respect perspectives I don’t agree with. To research deeply. To speak with confidence, even when uncertain. It’s reshaped how I think — not just in competition, but in life.
