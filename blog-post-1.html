The TSA Nationals Experience

Flying to Nationals, facing the unexpected, and learning more from losing than winning.

Heading to TSA Nationals felt like a dream. After months of prep and qualifying at states, I was finally in Louisville, surrounded by thousands of sharp, ambitious students in matching polos and badges. It was electric.

I competed in Forensic Science — an event that tested not just knowledge, but nerves. My partner and I had trained like crazy. We were ready. But then... we didn’t place.

I won't lie — it stung. But instead of walking away bitter, I stayed to watch other events. I talked to students who had competed for years. And I realized that what matters isn’t the medal. It’s the mindset. How you grow. How you take what didn’t work and make it better next time.

Since then, I’ve committed to improving — not just in content, but in composure, strategy, and teamwork. Nationals gave me more than a score. It gave me perspective.
