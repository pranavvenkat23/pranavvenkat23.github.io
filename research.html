Research

Antimicrobial Peptides and AI

As part of the George Mason Schar School BioScience Internship, I designed novel antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) using the GRAMPA database and applied machine learning models to predict their structure–activity relationship. This work integrates bioinformatics and computational biology to address real-world threats like antibiotic resistance. I am currently writing a manuscript for future publication and science competition submission (ISEF, JSHS).

Statistical Tools and Python Automation

Built custom Python scripts and used Pandas, NumPy, and scikit-learn for data wrangling, feature analysis, and model evaluation. This project enhanced my confidence in exploratory data analysis and reinforced my passion for interdisciplinary science and medicine.

Future Directions

I'm interested in expanding into neuroscience and disease modeling using AI, particularly in conditions like Parkinson's and Alzheimer's. I plan to integrate EEG or clinical data in future projects and explore formal mentorship, shadowing, or publication opportunities through school-year internships.
