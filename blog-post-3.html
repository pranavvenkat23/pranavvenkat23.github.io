Why I Love LD Debate

May 2025

I remember walking into my first Lincoln-Douglas debate round with shaking hands and a heart that beat like a drumline. Fast-forward a year, and I now lead our school’s LD team.

Debate taught me how to think, but more importantly, it taught me how to listen. Some of my best rounds weren’t won by fancy rhetoric — they were won by adapting on the fly, by understanding my opponent’s values better than they understood mine.

I’ve also learned that advocacy isn’t about being loud. It’s about being heard. And that’s something I’ll carry far beyond high school.
